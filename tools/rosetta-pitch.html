Honest footnotes

Cents are exact; culture is not. The 12-TET, Just, Sargam and Chinese lülü grids are well defined. But slendro and pelog are genuinely different on every gamelan — the numbers here are one representative averaging, easily off by ±40¢ from any real ensemble. Maqam is drawn on the 24-tone quarter-tone grid that the theory/notation uses, but performed neutral intervals drift (a neutral third often sits near 355¢, not a clean 350¢). Thai is idealised as 7 equal steps; real instruments vary. Turkish is the 53-comma (Holdrian) theoretical grid. The Just and Sargam ratios shown are common choices, not the only ones — classical shruti practice is raga-dependent. Treat this as a map, not the territory.
